Regions of the Solar System

  • Inner Planets: The rocky, terrestrial planets closest to the Sun (Mercury, Venus, Earth, Mars).
  • Asteroid Belt: A region of space between the orbits of Mars and Jupiter where most of the asteroids in our Solar System are found.
  • Outer Planets: The gas and ice giants that dominate the outer region (Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, Neptune).
  • Beyond: The icy regions of the Kuiper Belt and the distant Oort Cloud, home to dwarf planets and comets.
